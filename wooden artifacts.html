Product History

Wooden artifacts have a rich and diverse history that spans thousands of years, reflecting the cultural, technological, and artistic evolution of human societies. Here is a brief overview of the history of wooden artifacts:

  1. Prehistoric Period: Wooden artifacts can be traced back to prehistoric times when early humans used wood for essential tools and objects like spears, clubs, and digging sticks. Some of the earliest wooden artifacts date back to the Paleolithic era, around 2.5 million years ago, with the discovery of wooden implements in archaeological sites.
  2. Ancient Civilizations: In ancient civilizations such as Egypt, Mesopotamia, and China, wood was used for construction, transportation (boats and carts), and everyday objects like furniture, containers, and musical instruments.
  3. Classical Antiquity: In ancient Greece and Rome, wooden artifacts played a significant role in architecture, particularly in the construction of temples, theaters, and buildings
  4. Middle Ages: The Middle Ages saw a flourishing of wooden craftsmanship, with skilled artisans creating items like wooden chests, religious sculptures, and panel paintings.
